About Me

Hi There,

My name is Thi Bich Phuong Dao and I am currently pursuing an Early Childhood Education Diploma at Humber Polytechnic. Through my studies, I have built a strong foundation in child development, play-based learning, and inclusive education. My experiences in classroom observations, lesson planning, and interactive activities have allowed me to support children's growth in meaningful ways. I have also developed skills in responsive caregiving and positive guidance to create safe and engaging learning environments. I have a deep understanding of early learning frameworks and child-centered teaching approaches. I value collaboration with families and educators to provide hands-on, developmentally appropriate experiences that nurture children's curiosity, independence, and overall well-being.

Looking forward, I aim to create inclusive and supportive learning environments where all children feel valued and encouraged to explore. I am particularly passionate about integrating arts-based learning and creative expression into early education, using storytelling, music, and interactive play to enhance children's development. My goal is to make a lasting, positive impact by fostering a love for learning in a safe, engaging, and nurturing space.

Teaching & Learning Philosophy

Learning as a Journey

I believe teaching and learning are not a straight path, but an ongoing journey shaped by reflection, relationships, and experience. Children are capable, curious learners who build knowledge through exploration, play, and social interaction (Vygotsky, 1978). My role as an educator is to support rather than control learning by observing carefully, listening deeply, and offering guidance when needed (Dewey, 1938). I also believe families and communities are essential to children’s development because learning is deeply connected to relationships, culture, and lived experiences (Bronfenbrenner, 1979). The environment is another important teacher, as accessible materials and thoughtful spaces can invite independence, creativity, and engagement (Edwards et al., 2012). Overall, my philosophy is grounded in creating inclusive, responsive spaces where children feel safe, valued, and free to become themselves.

Curriculum & Pedagogy

1. Pedagogical Documentation

This piece of pedagogical documentation reflects how I try to slow down and truly listen to children's ideas in my daily practice. I chose to include this experience in my portfolio because it shows how children learn through play when they are given time, space, and trust. Instead of directing their play, I stayed present and followed their thinking as they explored building, balance, and creativity together. This experience connects to my belief that children are capable, curious, and full of potential, and that they construct knowledge through meaningful, hands-on experiences (Ontario Ministry of Education, 2014a). As an educator, I see my role as creating a supportive environment, asking open-ended questions, and extending learning while respecting children's independence and agency.

Continuous Professional Learning

1. Children

Goal:

In order to better understand and support children's thinking, I will intentionally use at least two open-ended questions during play each day, while paying close attention to how children respond, throughout my first three months in practice.

Strategies:

  • I will keep a small note on my phone or notebook of questions that feel natural to me so I can use them in the moment without interrupting the flow of play.
  • At the end of each week, I will reflect on which questions actually sparked conversation or thinking, and which ones felt forced, so I can adjust my approach.

2. Families

Goal:

In order to build genuine and trusting relationships with families, I will make a consistent effort to connect with each family at least once a week through small, meaningful interactions or updates during my first three months.

Strategies:

  • I will share short, real moments from the child's day (not just general updates) so families can feel more connected to their child's experiences.
  • I will take time to listen to families' perspectives and slowly incorporate what I learn about their child into my daily interactions.

3. Colleagues

Goal:

In order to grow more confident in my role and learn from others, I will initiate at least one reflective conversation each week with a colleague about what I am noticing in the classroom during my first three months.

Strategies:

  • I will be honest about what I am unsure about and ask for feedback instead of trying to figure everything out on my own.
  • I will also share my own observations and ideas, even if they are not fully formed, to build more open and collaborative communication.

4. Communities

Goal:

In order to help children feel more connected to the world around them, I will introduce at least one element from the community (such as materials, stories, or experiences) each month that reflects their lives and backgrounds during my first three months.

Strategies:

  • I will pay attention to what children talk about (places, people, experiences) and use that as a starting point to bring the community into the classroom.
  • I will explore simple ways to include diverse and meaningful materials that reflect different cultures and experiences in an authentic way.

5. Environment

Goal:

In order to create a space that truly reflects children's interests, I will intentionally make small changes to the environment each week based on what I observe in their play during my first three months.

Strategies:

  • I will focus on observing before changing anything, so that my decisions are based on what children are actually interested in, not what I assume.
  • After making changes, I will watch how children respond and use that to decide whether to keep, adjust, or remove materials.
